The best organizations run on a single, continuously updated picture of reality: what was decided, what is actually true, and who owns what. When that picture is clear and shared, decisions stick, priorities stay sharp, and execution matches intent.

Most companies don't have it.

They have dashboards, transcripts, wikis, and Slack threads but no reliable system that keeps everything connected and truthful as reality changes. The result is drift: decisions get re-litigated, priorities multiply, and the organization slowly loses its grip on what matters.

I help growth-stage and AI-native companies (Series B–E) design internal operating systems that preserve this clarity as they scale.

In practice, this shows up in Chief of Staff, Strategy & Operations, and Business Operations roles inside companies building or adopting AI systems.

These tools are prototypes that demonstrate how I approach organizational problems: identify where decision-making breaks down, trace it to missing or fragmented context, and build the mechanism that reconnects it.

These are working prototypes of an internal operating layer, not standalone products.

In practice, these capabilities would be embedded directly into the systems teams already use: Salesforce, Linear, Notion, Slack, and meeting systems, surfacing insights inside existing workflows rather than as separate applications.

The Arc

Three companies.
One pattern underneath all of them.

Across Artsy, ASAPP, and Braze, the surface problems changed. The underlying pattern didn't.

Each company had strong people, strong tools, and strong data. And still struggled with alignment over time.

In every case, the constraint wasn't information or intelligence. It was that context lived in fragments. Different teams saw different versions of truth, and there was no system that continuously reconciled them.

The Argument

Why organizations accumulate execution debt despite having more information than ever

The assumption was that access to information would solve alignment. Companies now have more data than ever, but decision quality hasn't improved at the same rate.

"The real constraint is what happens after information appears: how it is interpreted, turned into decisions, assigned ownership, and kept aligned as reality changes. On that front, we built almost nothing."

The real constraint is how information is interpreted, how decisions are made, how ownership is assigned, and how alignment is maintained as conditions change. Most companies have built systems to store information, but few have built systems to act on it consistently.

As a result, goals multiply without hierarchy, metrics proliferate but lose trust, and meetings fail to generate decisions people act on. Ownership becomes implicit, and companies stop running on a shared picture of reality. Instead, they start running on informal memory and repeated clarification.

AI increases the volume of output every team can generate. Without a shared context layer, that only increases fragmentation: more decisions, more artifacts, more systems, but no improvement in coherence. The bottleneck shifts from production to alignment, which is the layer I'm focused on building.
